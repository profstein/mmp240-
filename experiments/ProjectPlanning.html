Project Planning &
Creative Brief

Professor Chris Stein

The Client

Questions to Answer about the Client

  • Who is the Client?

  • Why do they want the site?

  • What do they want the Audience to get from the site?

The Audience (users)

Questions to Answer about the Audience

  • What are their goals (why would they come to the site)?

  • Who are they?

  • Do they have an existing relationship with the Client?

The Content and Interactivity

Questions to Answer about the Content

  • What content specifically will be on the site (content inventory)?

  • Besides navigation, what interactive features will be on the site?

  • Who will provide the content? And does it exist already, if not when?

Navigation

Questions to Answer about Navigation

  • What pages will be on the site?

  • What is the way those pages are ordered and linked to each other

Documenting Navigation

  • Sitemaps are the most common form of documentation. Sitemaps usually just show visually how pages are linked through the navigation.

Content Organization

Questions to Answer about Content Organizatoin

  • What content and interactivity specifically will be on each page?

Documenting Content Organization

  • Wireframes are the most common form of documentation. More detailed projects require high fidelity or annotated wireframes and also additional writing.

Creative Brief

Questions the Creative Brief Should Answer

  • How does the site meet the client goals?

  • How does the site meet the audience goals?

  • What is the plan for page navigation and content?

  • How will the site be made? (creative process)

  • Who will make the site? (team and roles)

  • How will the final product be delivered?

  • What is the timeline (with deliverables)?

  • What is the cost?
