Example Do’s and Don’ts

Text in Green is for do's and text in Red is for don'ts

(almost) Never set height.

nav and footer are OK sometimes, but avoid if possible

Giant Navigation

Don’t let the nav take over the screen

Use a Color Scheme

It should help both to communicate information and help visual design

Repeat Color Scheme

But watch how much space you're using

Default widths are for small screens

This is one of reasons to do mobile first. It’s easier to adjust to larger screens.

Use a Typographic Hierarchy, Space out Sections

Poor Typographic Hierarchy Example

Example with Poor Spacing

Use Icons

To help separate sections and help scanability. These icons could be a bit smaller and sized the same.

Try to fit sections on a screen

This isn’t always possible and shouldn’t be forced. But if you can …

Use Backgrounds and Textures Carefully

They should enhance the design and give it depth, not take up too much room from the content on small screens.

Goldilocks Image Sizes

Don’t make them too big (unless they are the main content) or too small.

One or Two Columns

Genearlly stick with 1 or two columns on small screen.
